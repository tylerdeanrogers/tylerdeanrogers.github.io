Teaching and Learning Center

Rebranding an essential service for University of Oregon students

The Problem:

The University’s learning services department provides a ton of resources for students and teachers on campus, but the brand and website was struggling to connect with its audience. The logo was dated and lacked consistency across various media, the website was created before there was even a need, much less a solution for mobile compatibility, and overall the UO TLC brand needed some TLC (I’m sorry I couldn’t resist).

The Solution:

An updated logo that works well for print and digital, big and small, and black and white. When you’re in a college town it seems like nearly every brand piggy backs on the university brand. Consequently you see tons of green and yellow. When choosing the color scheme for the TLC we were determined to keep it relatable to Oregon without getting lumped into the huge pool of monotonous green.

The Website:

The old website was a jumbled mess of text and links. The content was useful, but unorganized and overwhelming. The UX for this site was challenging, but after many hours of working with the client to trim the fat I think we've come up with an orgazanized and functional resource for students.
